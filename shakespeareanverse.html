Romeo and Juliet
The earth, that’s nature’s mother, is her tomb.
What is her burying, grave that is her womb.
And from her womb children of divers kind
We sucking on her natural bosom find,
Many for many virtues excellent,
None but for some and yet all different.
Oh, mickle is the powerful grace that lies
In herbs, plants, stones, and their true qualities.
For naught so vile that on the earth doth live
But to the earth some special good doth give.
Nor aught so good but, strained from that fair use
Revolts from true birth, stumbling on abuse(Shakespeare 2.3.9-20).

Shakespeare said it too. We who depend on the Earth for nourishment will find herbs, plants, and stones filled with great virtue and power yet all have their own special gift. But if something is misused and abused, it will turn bad. Lesson: Don't misuse the Earth, and let's appreciate all the gifts the Earth bestows to us!
